Let $p$ be a prime number. Let $\alpha\in \Z$. Fermat's little theorem states that, if $p$ does not divide $\alpha$, then
\[
  \alpha^{p-1}\equiv 1\pmod p.
\]
Therefore,
\[
  \beta^p \equiv \beta\pmod p
\]
for all integers $\beta$.
