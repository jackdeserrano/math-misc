Let
\begin{center}
\begin{tikzcd}
\phi : A\ar[r] & B
\end{tikzcd}
\end{center} 
be a ring homomorphism. Any left $B$-module $M$ can be given the structure of a left $A$-module by setting $a\cdot m = \phi (a)\cdot m$. One denotes this $A$-module $M_{[\phi]}$.

If $\Lambda$ is an $A$-module, a homomorphism
\begin{center}
\begin{tikzcd}
u : \Lambda\ar[r]& M_{[\phi]}
\end{tikzcd}
\end{center}
is, therefore, a homomorphism of abelian groups such that $u(a\cdot\lambda) = \phi(a)\cdot u(\lambda)$ for $a\in A$ and $\lambda\in \Lambda$. This is a \defn{$\phi$-homomorphism}
\begin{center}
\begin{tikzcd}
\Lambda\ar[r] & M,
\end{tikzcd}
\end{center} 
and the pair $(\phi, u)$ is a \defn{dihomomorphism} of $(A,\Lambda)$ in $(B, M)$.

\footnotesize
See EGA I 1.0.2.
